Presentation Requirements

Presentation Requirements for AfIHC 2024

Please take note of the presentation requirements below carefully.

Note

Note 1

All abstracts must be submitted in English.

Note 2

Participants are responsible for registration, travel, meals, and hotel costs.

Note 3

Abstracts or proposals that do not meet the required format will not be considered.

Note

Note 4

Kindly indicate whether your abstract submission is for a virtual presentation only.

Note 5

Please indicate at the bottom of your abstract whether you would like to give an oral, poster or panel presentation.

Note 6

Any presenter who is unable to attend can arrange for another qualified individual to present the paper/poster in question.

Note

Note 7

Submission of an abstract constitutes permission for the conference organizers to publish it in either printed or electronic format for conference promotional purposes.

Note 8

All abstracts submitted will be considered eligible for one type of presentation: oral, poster or panel. The decision of the Abstract Review Committee regarding acceptance and presentation of abstracts is final.

Note 9

Where hopeful presenters have submitted multiple abstracts, the scientific committee will make best efforts to ensure that presenters are not invited to present more than two abstracts.

Note

Note 10

Abstracts not exceeding 250 words for oral and poster presentation and 400 words for panel presentation must be submitted by email with the subject “AfIHC 2024” to abstract@afihc.org or uploaded on our website in pdf or doc format using the abstract template.

Note 11

Panel presentations must span an entire session and not exceed 1.5 hours. Panel proposals must include session title, description, participants, format, audio-visual equipment needs. Proposal descriptions must clearly state objectives of the presentation and demonstrate relevance to one of the sub-themes of the conference.

Conference Theme

AfIHC 2024 Sub-themes

Below are the subthemes for AfIHC 2024

  1. Mental health
  2. Burden of chronic diseases
  3. Climate change and health
  4. Healthcare policy and financing
  5. Gender-based violence and health implications
  6. Education and training for healthcare professionals
  7. Sexual and reproductive health including HIV and AIDS
  8. Impact of food insecurity and water insecurity on health
  9. Progress on achieving the sustainable development goals (SDGs)
  10. Technology and innovations including telemedicine and telehealth
  11. The role of social sciences in strengthening Africa's healthcare system
  12. Response strategies to emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases
  13. Cultural competence and community engagement in healthcare delivery
  14. Medical devices, circular economy, and environmentally sustainable healthcare practices in Africa and globally

Presentation Preference

  • Oral
  • Poster
  • Panel or Pre-conference workshop

Abstract Structure

Consider including the following information in the abstract, when relevant (300 word limit):

  • Background/Introduction
  • Purpose
  • Methods
  • Results
  • Conclusions

Title

The maximum title length should be limited to 15 words.

Submission Options

Submission Options For AfIHC 2024

Please take note of the submission options below carefully.

Oral Presentations

Oral presentations provide authors the opportunity to present original work related to any of the conference sub-themes. Oral presentations will have 15-minute time slots and 5 minutes for questions from the audience.

Submit abstract here

Poster Presentations

Our poster session provides an interactive forum for authors to discuss their original work related to any of the conference sub-themes. Submissions should represent original empirical research, theoretical development,or reviews.

Submit abstract here

Panel Presentations

Panel presentations provide authors the option to organize a panel comprising multiple (but related) presentations. Each panel presentation session will be 60 minutes long, with 50 minutes for the presentation and 10 minutes for discussion.
